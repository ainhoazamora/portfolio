About me

Hi, I'm Ainhoa Zamora Martínez!

I'm a criminology researcher with a strong focus on computational methods and data-driven analysis. My work bridges criminological theory with advanced machine learning techniques to uncover patterns in complex social phenomena.

Currently pursuing an International Master's in Advanced Research in Criminology (IMARC), I specialize in applying predictive modeling and explainable AI to criminological research. My technical toolkit includes ensemble machine learning methods such as XGBoost and Random Forest for classification and prediction tasks, alongside network analysis approaches using Hierarchical Bayesian Spatio Temporal (HBST) to map relational dynamics in criminal networks.

With a First-Class Honours degree in Criminology from the University of Bristol and hands-on experience as a Fraud Analyst, I bring both theoretical rigor and practical application to my research, focusing on a wide rage of areas such as human and organ trafficking, financial crime, AI and machine learning, computational criminology and border security.
